# Social Control Plane — Playbook

Status: Active working playbook
Updated: 2026-04-13

## Purpose
This is the operating playbook for repeatable social production inside SCP.

Use it when an agent or human is turning long-form content into drafts, media, and scheduled posts.

---

## LinkedIn article-led carousel system

### When to use it
Use this format when the goal is:
- drive traffic back to a long-form article
- teach one sharp idea to a construction audience
- make the post feel native to LinkedIn instead of like an ad banner

Do **not** use this format for every post. It is a strong format, not the only format.

### Default shape
- 4 slides
- `1080x1080`
- sequence: `cover -> bullets/problem -> bullets/solution -> CTA`
- article URL in post body for traffic-driving batches
- post body formatted as one compact paragraph for LinkedIn preview efficiency

### Audience rules
Write for:
- contractors
- supers
- PMs and PEs
- architects and designers curious about AI in operations

Do not write for developers.
Do not drift into prompt-engineering talk.
Do not sound like SaaS LinkedIn sludge.

### Visual rules
- use real construction or jobsite-adjacent imagery
- avoid generic laptop/office stock unless the article is specifically about office workflow
- Contractor-AI logo and tagline may float on the image, they do **not** need a footer box
- CTA labels should float or underline when possible, not default to pills and boxes
- avoid repeated support lines across multiple covers
- if a design element starts feeling templated, kill it

### Copy rules
- cover headline must make the point fast
- subhead should sharpen the claim, not restate it
- support line should be specific to that article, never reused batch-wide
- bullet slides should speak in operator language
- CTA slide should point to the article clearly without looking like a banner ad

## Approved layout families

The rule is variation with discipline.
Do not copy-paste one layout across every carousel in a batch.

### Family A — Panel-led cover
Best for: strong thesis, practical operator advice

- dark left content panel
- full-bleed lifestyle/jobsite image on right
- floating Contractor-AI lockup at bottom
- bullets on dark field with narrow image slice
- CTA on dark card over image

Use when the headline is the hero.

### Family B — Image-first cover
Best for: field-heavy topics, schedule/rework/risk themes

- image dominates 60-70% of slide
- headline sits lower or centered on a smaller dark block
- less copy on cover, more visual tension
- bullet slides can use stacked cards over a more visible photo
- CTA should feel lighter than Family A

Use when the photo carries emotional weight.

### Family C — Split-band cover
Best for: comparisons, myths, mistakes, “what goes wrong / what works” topics

- headline area in a horizontal or vertical band
- secondary text separated more aggressively from title
- bullets can alternate card density or icon treatment
- CTA can use underlined label + floating URL instead of boxed URL lockup

Use when contrast and pacing matter more than drama.

### Rotation rule
For any batch larger than 3 carousel posts:
- use at least 2 layout families
- prefer 3 families in a 9-post batch
- do not run more than 2 posts in a row with the same family

## Review checklist for LinkedIn carousels

Before approving:
- Does this feel like Tim, not a content team?
- Does the first slide earn the swipe?
- Is the imagery construction-relevant?
- Did we avoid generic repeated phrases?
- Is the logo treatment clean and unobtrusive?
- Does the CTA fit inside the layout cleanly?
- Does the set feel like a system without looking duplicated?

If the answer to the last question is no, make a variant.

---

## What we learned from the 2026-04-13 Contractor-AI batch

### What worked
- square LinkedIn-native carousels beat old banner-style media
- real construction imagery worked better than generic office visuals
- floating brand lockup worked better than boxed footer branding
- underlined floating CTA labels worked better than pills when space got tight
- compact single-paragraph LinkedIn body copy preserved more preview real estate

### What did not work
- repeated support lines across covers
- boxed footer treatment on every slide
- boxed CTA label when the text fit was tight
- anything that felt like one template cloned 9 times

### Hard rule going forward
A reusable system is good.
A visibly duplicated batch is bad.

Build systems that generate variation, not systems that stamp out sameness.
